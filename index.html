Positioning

I like the test bench part of engineering.

I am an Electrical Engineering student at UC Davis focused on the handoff between physical hardware and robotics software: firmware timing, communication reliability, board-level behavior, and control interfaces.

One of my greatest strengths is my perseverance and inherent curiosity for design. I enjoy designing tests and iterating on findings in both software and hardware. Finding issues and polishing them out is what I strive to do, ensuring that the system at hand runs as smoothly as possible. I take initiative to flesh out problems that may arise, going beyond the assigned task and making my contribution as complete as possible.

Krysalis Robotic Hand

Public-safe case study from LARA work on a humanoid robotic hand. My contribution sits at the firmware and electronics layer: stabilizing communication paths, diagnosing I2C bus behavior, and connecting board-level decisions to physical robot reliability.

BCI / Python decoder / actuator safety

BCI Prosthetic Leg Control

Public robotics/BCI portfolio project for translating EEG motor-imagery features into safe actuator trigger commands for a prosthetic-leg test rig.

  • Built live OpenBCI/Cyton decoding with BrainFlow, filtering, CSP feature extraction, and LDA scoring.
  • Added dry-run-first safety gates, thresholding, refractory timing, and REST-trial output blocking.
  • Published replay reports that separate in-session signal quality from cross-session generalization risk.

A published paper connecting AI decision-making, transparency, and moral responsibility in autonomous driving systems.

Journal of Student Research / 2024

The Implementation of Ethical AI Within Autonomous Vehicles

Vadim Fedorov and Noah T. Curran. Journal of Student Research, 13(1). DOI: 10.47611/jsrhs.v13i1.5973.

Abstract summary: This paper examines the ethical implications of AI-driven autonomous vehicles in high-stakes road scenarios. It compares real-world incidents with theoretical decision cases, questions how opaque algorithms should handle human safety tradeoffs, and argues for clearer ethical guidelines rooted in deontological reasoning, safety, individual autonomy, and public trust.
